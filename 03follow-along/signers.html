Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

The Declaration of Independence, adopted on July 4, 1776, was signed by 56 delegates from the 13 American colonies. Some of the most famous signers, due to their roles in shaping the United States or their lasting historical recognition, include

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress, known for his bold signature.
Thomas Jefferson
Primary author of the Declaration, later third U.S. President.
Benjamin Franklin
Statesman and diplomat, helped refine the document and secure French support.
John Adams
Advocate for independence, later second U.S. President.
Samuel Adams
Patriot and Boston Tea Party organizer, key in colonial resistance.

These men stood out for their leadership, contributions to the Revolution, or later prominence. Others, like Roger Sherman, Robert Morris, or Button Gwinnett, are less famous but were vital to the process. Each signer risked treason charges, making their commitment notable.
