History of Body Weight Athletics

The Beginning of Rock Climbing

Late 19th Century Rock Climbing Originated as a sport in Europe, particularly in the Alps, with early mountaineering.

1950s–1960s Gained traction in the U.S. with pioneers like John Gill focusing on bouldering, emphasizing athleticism.

1980s–1990s Indoor climbing gyms emerged, making the sport more accessible and boosting popularity.

2000s–Present Exploded with urban gyms, competitive events, and inclusion in the 2020 Olympics, driving mainstream appeal. Social media and films like *Free Solo* further amplified interest.

The History of Calisthenics & Bodyweight Exercising

  1. The History of Calisthenics

    The Beginning of Calisthenics

    Ancient Times Calisthenics Originated ancient Greece for fitness and aesthetics (e.g., Spartan training).

    19th Century Formalized in Europe and America for school and military fitness programs.

    Early 20th Century Faded as weightlifting and gym culture rose.

    2010s–Present Revived via street workout culture, YouTube, and fitness influencers emphasizing structured bodyweight routines.

  2. The History of BodyWeight Exercising

    The Beginning of BodyWeight Exercising

    Ancient Times Bodyweight ExercisesUsed in ancient China and India (e.g., yoga, martial arts training).

    19th Century Incorporated into early fitness systems, often overlapping with calisthenics.

    Mid-20th Century Eclipsed by gym-based training trends.

    2000s–Present Gained popularity with minimalist fitness trends, CrossFit, and accessible home workouts, especially during COVID-19 lockdowns.
